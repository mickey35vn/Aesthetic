BroadBand Light (BBL™)

Has the Fountain of Youth come to Nob Hill Medical & Aesthetics? Perhaps it has, in the form of Forever Young Broadband Light (BBL) Therapy.

Sciton BroadBand Light (BBL™) treatments can change the expression of genes associated with the aging process to more closely resemble young skin. A 2012 Stanford University study published in the Journal of Investigative Dermatology supports the theory that skin aging can be altered at a cellular level, rather than just a cosmetic imitation of youthful appearance.

BBL treatments are requested by patients for promoting a more youthful and rejuvenated appearance. BBL targets treatment for many skin conditions including acne, pigmented lesions, vascular lesions and hair removal.

The procedure is relatively quick. Depending on the size of the skin area being treated, and the seriousness of the condition, most BBL treatments last between a few minutes to a half hour. An additional bonus of BBL is minimal recovery time. Some swelling and redness may occur, but typically subsides by the following day.

It is possible to delay skin aging with 2-4 scheduled Forever Young BBL Treatments per year. Schedule your appointment with Nob Hill Medical & Aesthetic today.

Common Questions About BBL (BroadBand Light)

What is BroadBand Light?

BroadBand Light (BBL) or Intense Pulse Light (IPL) uses a high-powered computer-controlled flashgun to deliver an intense visible, broad-spectrum pulse of light, generally in the visible spectral range of 400 to 1200 nm.

What is the downtime for BBL?

Treatments have no significant downtime and only require a brief period of reduced sun exposure before and after treatments.

Is BBL only used on faces?

BBL treatments can not only rejuvenate the skin of the face, but also the neck, chest, arms, hands, back and other skin of concern.

What can BBL treat or improve?

It can treat many skin conditions including acne, pigmented lesions, such as freckles and age spots, and vascular lesions, such as cherry angiomas, telangiectasias, and rosacea. It can be also used for hair removal.
Sciton BBL treatments actually improve DNA damage in deeper layers of skin, slowing the changes of aging and causing skin to behave and look as it did when it was younger.

Is BBL a good treatment for acne?

When performed with the more powerful Sciton system, BBL is one of the most exciting therapies that can help treating acne, as well as the flushing and redness of rosacea.

How long do BBL results last?

Best results usually require a series of 3-4 treatments, depending on the degree of damage to be repaired. Treatments can also be combined with skin tightening modalities, microlaserpeels or fractional resurfacing treatments for even more dramatic results.

How old are most people who start to get BBL?

Anybody who has photodamage is a good candidate for BBL. Many patients start getting BBL treatments when they are in their early 30s.

Are there any side effects?

Just skin redness which is very mild.

How long does the total face treatment take?

Only about 15-20 minutes.

Does it hurt?

There is a little discomfort, but not enough for any numbing to be necessary.

Is BBL all done in one appointment?

Yes. Patients can come in for a consultation and have the procedure done the same day.
